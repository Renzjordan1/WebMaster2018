COURSES

The following courses are MST specific

MST Geometry

Grade Level: 9th

Prerequisite(s): Admission to MST, Placement test

This course is an enriched Algebra 2 curriculum with an in-depth study of equations and functions. It's designed for students who have completed 8th grade Algebra 1 and Advanced Geometry. This course includes step functions, natural logarithms, determinants, and conic sections.

MST Physics

Grade Level: 9th

Prerequisite(s): Admission to MST

This course aims to give 1st year MST students the foundations needed to be successful in rigor and research throughout their high school career. Students also learn introductory framework and fundamental laboratory skills for future science courses.

Advanced Placement Computer Science Principles

Grade Level: 9th

Prerequisite(s): None

This course introduces students to the main ideas of computer science, teaching the ideas and practices of computational thinking and inviting students to understand computing's effects on the world. This course promotes deep learning of computational content, develops computational thinking skills and engages students' creativity.

MST Algebra 2

Grade Level: 10th

Prerequisite(s): Successful completion of MST Geometry

This course allows students opportunities to make conjectures about geometric situations and prove in multiple ways to form a logical conclusion of their thinking. The course employs integrating synthetic, transformational and coordinate approaches, justify relationships and properties and investigate geometric situations. It heavily stresses advanced algebra, the theory of functions, trigonometry and statistics.

MST Chemistry

Grade Level: 10th

Prerequisite(s): MST Geometry and/or concurrent

This course, using the themes of research and problem solving, explores topics such as equations, equilibrium kinetics, thermodynamic relationships, chemical calculations and descriptive particles. Students will design and carry out an original research project.

Programming Introduction

Grade Level: 10th

Prerequisite(s): Technology Competency and Algebra 1

This course covers the basic concepts of computer science: data representation and encapsulation, substitution, composition of functions, iterations, recursions, and algorithmic analysis. Students start with no programming knowledge, and learn to write programs using a modern, high-level programming language. Development of problem solving skills is stressed.

MST Pre-Calculus/Calculus

Grade Level: 11th

Prerequisite(s): MST Algebra 2

This course explores topics like advanced theory of functions, emphasizing graphing techniques, algebraic theory, advanced geometry and trigonometry, limits and introducing differential calculus.

MST Biology

Grade Level: 11th

Prerequisite(s): MST Integrated Science and MST Chemistry(can take concurrently)

This course includes concepts in biochemistry, cellular structure, function and reproduction, photosynthesis, respiration, genetics, classification, metabolic physiology, and ecology. Students also design, carry out and complete a research project in a MST field of their choosing.

Advanced Placement Calculus B/C

Grade Level: 12th

Prerequisite(s): MST Pre-Calculus/Calculus

Concepts include conceptual understanding of definite integrals, Riemann sums, the fundamental theorem of calculus, integration methods, differential equations, applications of definite integrals, convergence of infinite series, power series, Taylor/Maclaurin polynomials, parametric curves, polar functions.

MST Discrete Math

Grade Level: 12th

Prerequisite(s): Calculus

This course includes advanced business and computer applications, with a variety of discrete topics and strategies like set theory, mathematical induction, graph theory, permutations and combinations, etc. Can be taken by students who still need to meet the state requirement for a math course yearly.

Advance Program Linear Algebra

Grade Level: 12th

Prerequisite(s): Calculus

This course studies topics in Linear Algebra, and possibly material from multivariable Calculus. The course will cover matrix theory and linear algebra, which are useful in other areas. Linear algebra studies systems of linear equations and the properties of matrices.

Advanced Placement Biology

Grade Level: 11th-12th

Prerequisite(s): Successful completion of Biology 1

This course covers topics usually covered in college courses for majors. This course follows the College Board curriculum.

Advanced Placement Chemistry

Grade Level: 11th-12th

Prerequisite(s): Successful completion (B or higher) in ADV Chemistry or MST Chemistry

This course covers materials that are normally covered in college introduction courses, following the College Board curriculum.

Advanced Placement Environmental Science

Grade Level: 11th-12th

Prerequisite(s): Integrated Science (or Chemistry), Biology

This course provides students with the scientific principles, concepts, and methodologies needed to understand the cycles in nature, interrelationships of the world, and the ability to find and analyze environmental problems.

Advanced Placement Physics 1

Grade Level: 11th-12th

Prerequisite(s): Advanced or MST Pre-Calculus

This course is algebra=based, introducing college-level topics like Newtonian mechanics; work, energy, and power; mechanical waves and sound and simple circuits. Using inquiry-based learning, students will develop scientific critical thinking and reasoning skills.

Advanced Placement Physics 2

Grade Level: 12th

Prerequisite(s): AP Physics Part 1

This course is algebra=based, introducing college-level topics like fluid statics and dynamics; thermodynamics with kentic energy; PV diagrams and probability; eledtrostatics; more advanced circuits, magnetic fields; electromagnetism; physical and geometric optics; and quantum, atomic, and nuclear physics. Using inquiry-based learning, students will develop scientific critical thinking and reasoning skills.

Web Page Development: JavaScript 1

Grade Level: 10th-12th

Prerequisite(s): Computer applications

This course focuses on using HTML to create web pages. Topics include creating web documents with a simple text editor, using a simple web editor, and features like layout, tables, images, forms, frames and adding sound and video. It also includes developing site specifications and methods to increase the appeal of web sites, preparing web documents appropriate for business and professional use, and the use of CSS and W3C in web design.

Web Site Design 2

Grade Level: 11th-12th

Prerequisite(s): Web Page Development: JavaScript

This course teaches students web page development software to edit web pages, insert text content, graphics, and interactive modules. Students also learn using research software tools to design the look, feel, and navigation of a web page, as well as web page updates and using associated hardware.

Advanced Placement Computer Science A: Java

Grade Level: 11th-12th

Prerequisite(s): Computer applications, Programming Introduction

This course teaches the basics common to most first-year sequence in college computer science majors. The focus is primarily on object-oriented programming, including inheritance and modularization. It also includes common data structures; heaps, stacks, queues, linked lists, binary search trees, and hash maps.

Cyber Science

Grade Level: 11th-12th

Prerequisite(s): None

This course provides innovative database analysis, business intelligence, decision support, production reporting and rapid application development skills. Students learn how to break data barriers and free information to help users gain an advantage.

Advanced Placement Research

Grade Level: 11th-12th

Prerequisite(s): Successful completion of Advanced Placement Seminar

This course allows students to deeply explore an academic topic of their choosing. Then students design, plan, and conduct a year-long research based investagation to answer a research question.

Special Note: The requirements and grade level aren't absolute. They aren't necessary to be able to take the class. However, this doesn't mean the requirements can be negated. They can fluctuate, but not ignored. Ask our student counselors if you have questions.
